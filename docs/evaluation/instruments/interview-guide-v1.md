# Follow-Up Interview Guide v1

Use this guide for lightweight post-cycle qualitative follow-up.
Keep the structure stable across cycles.

## Interview metadata

- participant id
- cycle id or cycles discussed
- condition discussed
- interviewer
- date

## Core questions

1. What stood out most to you about this cycle?
2. Did the amount of material you saw feel manageable, too thin, or too heavy?
3. Did you encounter any perspective that changed, sharpened, or complicated your view?
4. Did the exchange feel thoughtful, reactive, flat, or something else?
5. If you were in the intervention condition: did the routing or explanation feel helpful, confusing, or paternalistic?
6. If the cycle felt quieter than a normal thread, did that feel healthier or less engaging?
7. What would have made the cycle easier or more worthwhile to participate in?

## Interviewer notes

- Probe for concrete examples rather than general impressions.
- Do not lead participants toward a positive or negative judgment.
- Record when a participant distinguishes overload from boredom.
- Record when a participant distinguishes clarity from paternalism.
